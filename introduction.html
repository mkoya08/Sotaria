The Beginning
Observing the World
Soteria has ice then there is the ocean underneath and eventually you reach the mantle and the planet's core. The shifting tectonic plates creates expansive mountains and underwater volcanoes. Seawater rushes into cracks in the ocean crust, is heated by magma and rushes out at high temperatures which creates hydrothermal vents. Soteria careful tethers on the edge of the habitable zone of its solar system. Also, maintaining a strong planetary magnetosphere has helped to sustain the water. - 4/5/2024 9:11 EST, Logan
